Description

The Embraer EMB-312 Tucano is a turboprop basic and advanced training aircraft developed by Embraer in the late 1970s. It was designed primarily for military pilot training, offering good performance, low operating costs, and excellent handling characteristics.

Unlike the A-29 Super Tucano, the EMB-312 is not optimized for combat. It lacks heavy armor, advanced sensors, and reinforced structures for attack missions. Some versions can carry light weapons for training or secondary roles, but its main role has always been as a trainer aircraft.

The A-29 Super Tucano is a direct evolution of the EMB-312, featuring a more powerful engine, strengthened airframe, modern avionics, and true combat capability.
